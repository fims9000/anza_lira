## 7) Ready paragraph (insert into Results/Discussion)

`In the cross-domain protocol, the same anisotropic fuzzy local aggregation block exhibits different operating regimes. On HRF_SegPlus, AZ-Thesis improves overlap quality against baseline (Dice +0.0364, IoU +0.0408), mainly by increasing precision. On Roads_HF, both variants are near parity in Dice/IoU, while AZ preserves slightly better structural consistency (clDice +0.0017). On SpaceNet3, the initial AZ configuration was conservative, but after geometry-strength calibration the recovered run outperformed baseline (Dice +0.0448, IoU +0.0437). These results indicate that the geometric prior is transferable, while calibration should be adapted per domain.`
